When creating a character, the players will be able to choose several abilities made from pre-existing concepts, in order to put together one character build. 

Players will start with 5 Threads of Fate, and will be able to earn more as game goes on, and will be able to spend them at different times to unlock new abilities. 

Players can also take curses at the beginning in order to attain more Threads in exchange. Players can also not spend their Threads, to keep them in the campaign. 

Players start with:
Stats: Standart Array, +2 to any ability of their choice, and +1 to any ability of their choice.
Money: 100 Euros
Proficiencies: 2 skill proficiencies. Proficiency with simple weapons. 


## Thread abilities:

Tier I
- Standard Education: You gain proficiency in two skills of your choice, and one language of your choice. You also start with 100 more Euros.  
- Multilingual: You can read, write and speak two additional languages. (Can be taken multiple times)
- Keen Sight: You have advantage on Perception checks that rely on sight.
- Healthy I: You can use your bonus action to regain 1d8+CON hit points. You cannot use this ability again until you finish a long rest. 
- Backflip: As a bonus action, you can jump a number of feet equal to five times your proficiency bonus, without provoking opportunity attacks. You can use this trait only if your speed is greater than 0. You can use it a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.
- Resourceful Presence: You can pick up any amount of objects within 5ft of you as a bonus action. 
- Think Fast, Chucklenuts! : You can throw a light item you're holding as a bonus action. It counts as an improvised weapon. 
- Trash Bin Basketball enthusiast: You gain proficiency with improvised weapons.
- The Pen is Mightier Than The Sword: You gain a +2 damage bonus to improvised weapons. 
- Wealthy: You start with 200 extra Euros. (Can be taken multiple times)
- Mighty Punch: Your unarmed strike die increases by one tier (1->1d4->1d6->1d8->1d10->1d12). (Can be taken multiple times)
- First in Battle: You have advantage on initiative rolls.  
- Lightly Armored: You gain proficiency with light armor.
- Athlete: You gain a climbing speed equal to your walking speed. 
- Swimmer: You gain a swimming speed equal to your walking speed.
- Medium Armor Training: You gain proficiency with medium armor and shields. Can only be taken if you have proficiency with light armor. 
- Gun Training: You gain a gun proficiency. 
- Bow Training: You gain proficiency with longbows and crossbows. 
- Advanced Weapon Training: You gain proficiency with four martial weapons of your choice. 
Tier II
- Mighty: You fain +2 to any one ability of your choice. 
- Archery: You gain a +2 bonus to attack rolls you make with ranged weapons.
- Defense: You gain a +1 bonus to your AC
- Healthy II: You can use your bonus action to regain 1d10+CON hit points. You cannot use this ability again until you finish a short rest. 
- Dueling: When you are wielding a melee weapon in one hand, and no other weapons, you gain a +2 bonus to damage rolls with that weapon. 
- Weaponsmith: You can take one hour to create a simple weapon out of materials specifically gathered for the cause, worth at least 20 euros, as well as a toolbox. You can create a martial weapon within 10 hours, and by using materials worth at least 100 euros. You still need a toolbox.
- Expert I: You gain expertise in one skill of your choice. 
- Tumbler: Your training allows you to move through hostile creatures' spaces without penalty. Their square is not difficult terrain for you.

Tier III
- Built Different: You gain +1 to all abilities. 
- Cunning Action: You can use your bonus action to Dodge, Disengage or Dash. 
- Bulky Defense: While you aren't wearing armor and not wearing a shield, your AC equals 10 + your Dexterity modifier + your Constitution modifier. 
- Instinctive Defense: While you aren't wearing armor and not wearing a shield, your AC equals 10 + your Dexterity modifier + your Wisdom modifier.
- Expert II: You gain expertise in two skills of your choice. 
- Improved Critical: Your weapon attacks score a critical hit on a 19 or a 20. 
- Fighting Spirit: As a bonus action on your turn, you can give yourself advantage on all weapon attack rolls until the end of the current turn. When you do so, you also gain 5 temporary hit points. You can use this feature three times. You regain all expended uses of it when you finish a long rest.
- Dual Wielder: You gain a +1 bonus to AC while you are wielding a separate melee weapon in each hand. You can use two-weapon fighting even when the one-handed melee weapons you are wielding aren't light. You can draw or stow two one-handed weapons when you would normally be able to draw or stow only one.
- Mobile: Your speed increases by 10 feet. When you use the Dash action, difficult terrain doesn't cost you extra movement on that turn. When you make a melee attack against a creature, you don't provoke opportunity attacks from that creature for the rest of the turn, whether you hit or not.
- Savage Attacker: Once per turn when you roll damage for a melee weapon attack, you can reroll the weapon's damage dice and use either total. (The _Savage Attacker_ feat).

Tier IV
- Action Surge: On your turn, you can take one additional action. Once you use this feature, you must finish a short or long rest before you can use it again.
- Evasion: When you are subjected to an effect that allows you to make a Dexterity saving throw to take only half damage, you instead take no damage if you succeed on the saving throw, and only half damage if you fail.
- 

Tier V
- Rapid Strike: If you take the Attack action on your turn and have advantage on an attack roll against one of the targets, you can forgo the advantage for that roll to make an additional weapon attack against that target, as part of the same action. You can do so no more than once per turn.
- Extra Attack: You can attack twice, instead of once, whenever you take the Attack action on your turn.

Tier -I 
- Poor: You start with 5 Euros. You can't gain any more with any trait. 
- Slow I: Your speed is reduced by 10 feet. 
- Unlucky: You must reroll the first natural 20 you roll on a skill check, attack roll or saving throw each long rest. 
- Frail I: Your hit point maximum is reduced by 1 every level. 
- Illiterate: You cannot read or write any language. You can still speak languages you would normally know. You have disadvantage on all Intelligence Ability checks. 
- Anathema: Choose one creature type (e.g., undead, fiends, beasts). You have disadvantage on all attack rolls and saving throws against creatures of that type.

Tier -II
- Slow II: Your speed is reduced by 15 feet.
- Weak: All your abilities are reduced by one. 
- Frail II: Your hit point maximum is reduced by 2 every level. 

Tier -III
- Forever Linked to a Chair: You start in a wheelchair. Your movement speed becomes 5 feet when outside of the wheelchair, and you are automatically prone. It costs a full action to mount your wheelchair when you're not in it. While you're in the wheelchair, your movement speed becomes 10 feet, and can't be increased by any means. You have disadvantage on melee attack rolls with heavy weapons with. You can't move while holding a two-handed weapon. 
- Early Grave: When you fall below 0 hit points, you do not roll death saving throws. You die instantly. 
- Sickly Victorian Child: Your hit dice becomes a d1.

#Character 
A man, who created a [[Vibrations|Vibrational]] generator using a [[Nuclear]] engine in his backyard, in such a tone, that his body would get rapidly healed from any and all ailments in a radius around his house. This was thanks to the [[Dr Emoto's Water Experiments]], that he could achieve this. He was encountered by members of [[The Government]] of [[China]] after reports of [[Earthquake]]s reached them. They couldn't apprehend him, as at the time he had perfected his system to work with minimal vibrations. Any shot fired at him would regrow his body after a second, and even prolonged fire didn't seem to matter. Sadly, the continuous pain, and the ample adrenaline in his veins, as well as his madness, inherited from his achievement, caused him to beat 13 members of the [[The MSS]] in hand to hand combat with his fists, before they were forced to retreat from the location. 

Approximately 2 days after the retreat, and evacuation of the nearby city, [[The MSS]] dropped an anti tank shell on the nuclear engine in the backyard, causing it to detonate, and release radiation, as well as raw bits of Uranium in the area, causing the nearby city to be subjected to a massive localized earthquake, that ranked a 8.6 on the Richter Scale. 

Esero Khuga was caught in the reactor meltdown, causing his skin, bones, and other muscles to melt down, while rapidly healing, causing him to undergo extreme [[Mutation]]s, and absorbing the biomass in the area around his house. By the end of the earthquake, a whole city block, and then some was covered in a mass of flesh that was once the [[Body]] of [[Esero Khuga]]. Cancerous tumors, extra sets of eyes and teeth, bones where bones shouldn't be, extremities as high as buildings, as buildings themselves got converted into a part of Esero's new body. 

Esero is now known as [[RAB-Khuga]], a [[Cryptid]] and the only remaining inhabitant of the chinese village.

#Entity #Concept #Character #Cryptid 
## Things to Note:

- Formerly [[Esero Khuga]], a backyard scientist who achieved regenerative[[Immortallity]] through [[Vibrations|vibrational]] healing based on [[Dr Emoto's Water Experiments]].
    
- His original device was a [[Nuclear]]-powered vibrational generator, finely tuned to a frequency that caused rapid cellular regeneration in Esero's organism. 
    
- When [[The MSS]] destroyed his generator with an anti-tank shell, the resulting meltdown occurred simultaneously with his active regeneration. His body entered a state of **forced mitosis feedback loop**: cells destroyed by radiation would regenerate, only to be destroyed again, faster and faster, until the very concept of "[[Esero Khuga]]" became a continuous process rather than a discrete entity.
    
- The 8.6 magnitude [[Earthquake]] caused by the detonation was caused by his flesh growing faster than sound, and pushing against the earth, as well as the residual energy after the meltdown.
    
- Over approximately 47 minutes, his biomass expanded to consume:
    
    - His house and backyard (origin point)
        
    - 14 residential blocks
        
    - 3 schools (all evacuated)
        
    - 2 factories
        
    - 1 hospital (evacuation incomplete—see [[Missing Person]] files, Changsha Province, 2023)
        
    - The first 47 first responders who did not retreat in time
        
- The resulting entity is approximately **2.3 kilometers in diameter** at its widest point, though its shape is irregular and **still growing**, albeit at a slowed rate of approximately 1.2 meters per month.

- The Entity has several major arteries in it, perpetually causing a humming tune to emerge from each set of vocal cords, that keeps healing the entity, preventing any physical damage. 

- [[The Government]] of [[China]] has forbidden any further interaction or action against the entity, In a fear of accelerating it's development, or other unexpected mutations.
	- The usage of [[Nuke|Nuclear Weaponry]] against the entity is especially forbidden due to the effect closely mimicking the original event of expansion. 

- The creature is assumed to be in constant pain due to it's existence, and it has absorbed the [[Soul]]s of the 47 other individuals in the area, incorporating parts of their [[Body]] into the biomass, and achieving partially the effect of [[Incorruptible Saints|a saint]], causing the entity to obtain partial understanding of [[Magic]], as well as the ability to execute some simple enchantments and spells, now having larger areas of effect due to the area of the caster itself. 

- A group that has been stationed near the entity has repeatedly reported "Fireworks", as well as the sky above the entity "being set ablaze."
- If a [[Human]] or a similar creature touches the mass, it experiences rapid tumor growth, following up by a quick death. If they remain near the mass, they will be absorbed into the entity, and their soul will be used as an additional [[Mana]] battery. 

- There are several documented cases of "Limbs" extending from the mass, trying to grab at nearby creatures, and pulling them into the biomass. These limbs are mostly human hands, usually scaled much larger than the typical human hand, with the largest ones exceeding a length of 20 metres. There have been mentions of other tumorous growths, as well as several feet facing the air expanding into the air. 

- The eyes of the Biomass seem to follow around moving objects, even if they can't differentiate alive from non alive objects. 

- Three seperate cults have been identified to know about the mass itself. 
	- The [[Illuminati]] is the only cult that is named within this document. 

- [[The MSS]] has attempted:
    - Incendiary weapons
    - Chemical agents
    - Cryogenic freezing 
    - Precision nuclear strike (proposed, rejected)

- Since every single cell of the entity holds a fragment of the soul of Khuga, it means that he is experiencing all of his cells dying, is in extreme pain, and yet, is [[Immortallity|Immortal]], and unable to do anything about it.

#Concept
# Things to note:

- There’s a limited amount of souls in the world (due to the [[Bank of souls]])
    
- A person can be born without a soul, then they become [[Soulless]]
    

- There are always some spare souls, as when the souls run out, the end of the world will come. 
    

- There are cults of people with souls, that seek and eliminate other people with souls, to replenish the tree and to ward off the end of the world. 
    

- [[Methane]] is a byproduct of a soul being in a body, hence there can be no humans without methane, and to revive a soul, the body has to be full of methane, before a soul enters it.
    

- This means that the reason babies are born is because their mothers body produces some methane upon childbirth. How this could help you, I have no idea.
    

- When humans consume another entity, such as bread, or meat, that is when their body creates methane, as a way to get rid of the excess parts of the soul. This means that without feeding, you cannot have kids. 
    
- The souls have to spend an eternity inside of a heaven, or a hell, which is where the consciousness is left to rest forever, till they succumb to the nothingness, before being returned to the tree of life, for reincarnation. 
    
- Souls removed from the body can become ambient mana, if not redirected to heaven or hell. The usage of such mana chips away at them, meaning that mana is a renewable resource, but can run out. 
    

- Enough souls flying around can also get together to form a cryptid, or a ghost. 
    

- Souls can be removed from a body, and can enter a body. 
    

- They can be removed if they pass a certain threshold of damage received.
    
- A new soul can enter their place, if they overpower the body’s defenses, as well as the soul within it. Easier to do if the soul is weakened enough or if the body is empty.
    

- Normal souls are invisible when outside of a [[Human]] Body.

File: Theory D&D\Random Encounters\Food Rolls.md

THE D100

**1. The Mystery Can**  
No label. No idea. Could be soup. Could be cat food. Could be beets. Could be peaches. The only way to know is to open it. (DM decides later, or have them roll again on this table when they open it.)

**2. Warhead Sour Candy (Extreme)**  
A roll of six. The green ones are the worst. Your face does an involuntary thing. Your mouth will regret this tomorrow. Worth it.

**3. A Single Pickle**  
In a Ziploc baggie. Still floating in a little juice. Dill. Surprisingly crisp. Who saves one pickle?

**4. Saltine Cracker Sleeve**  
Crushed to absolute dust. You have a packet of saltine dust. You could make a slurry. You could inhale it. No good options.

**5. Grandma's Oatmeal Cookies**  
In a tin with flowers on it. Slightly too much cinnamon. They're falling apart a little, crumbs at the bottom. They're perfect. You feel guilty eating the last one.

**6. Gas Station Sushi**  
A terrible idea. A truly terrible idea. Why did anyone buy this? It's been in the roller case for hours, then sat overnight. The rice is weird. The fish is a suggestion. Con save to keep it down.

**7. Beef Jerky (Peppered)**  
Aggressively salty, aggressively chewy. Could double as a weapon in a pinch. The pepper flakes get stuck in your teeth for hours.

**8. Protein Bar (Cookies & Cream)**  
Tastes like candy pretending to be health food. Slightly chalky, but it'll keep you going. The white coating gets on your fingers.

**9. Honey Bun (Glazed)**  
Individually wrapped, impossibly sweet. Leaves a film of sugar on your fingers. The spiral is perfectly imperfect. A classic.

**10. Tuna Salad Kit**  
A small can of tuna, a separate packet of mayonnaise, a tiny plastic spoon, and a sleeve of crackers. Requires assembly. Smells exactly as you'd expect. Other party members will know what you're eating from three rooms away.

**11. MRE (Menu Unknown)**  
The label is gone. The pouch is olive drab. You open it. It's brown. It's fine. Comes with a tiny bottle of Tabasco, a cracker pack, and a spoon. The Tabasco is essential.

**12. Trail Mix (The Good Kind)**  
M&Ms, peanuts, cashews, raisins, and those little yogurt-covered pretzels. Everyone picks out the M&Ms first. Someone has already picked out the M&Ms. There are none left. You have been betrayed by a stranger.

**13. Peanut Butter Crackers**  
The kind with six little sandwiches and the plastic spreading knife. The knife is useless. You eat them in three bites. The peanut butter sticks to the roof of your mouth.

**14. An Actual Apple**  
Red Delicious. Slightly bruised. Real. Fresh. Crisp. This is a treasure beyond measure. You eat the whole thing, core included, like an animal.

**15. Bag of Oranges**  
Three of them. The net bag is torn. One is slightly soft. One is perfect. The smell alone lifts everyone's spirits. Vitamin C has never been so precious.

**16. Leftover Pizza Box**  
From two days ago. One slice left. Cheese. It's cold. The cheese has that slightly plasticky texture. It's still pizza. It's still good. You eat it standing up.

**17. A Full Sandwich (Kevin's)**  
Wrapped in deli paper with a name written in sharpie. "KEVIN." Turkey and Swiss on rye, lettuce, tomato, a little mustard. You don't know Kevin. Thanks, Kevin. You think about Kevin sometimes.

**18. MRE (Chili Mac)**  
Widely considered one of the better ones. Still brown. Still comes with Tabasco. The noodles are tiny. The chili is more of a suggestion. It's hot. It's food. It's okay.

**19. A Can of Tuna and One Packet of Crackers**  
The crackers are the kind that come with soup. Four tiny rectangles. You will make this work. You eat the tuna with the crackers, carefully rationing each cracker for maximum coverage.

**20. Freshly Baked Muffins**  
In a paper bag, still warm. Blueberry. Three of them. How? Why? Who left these? You don't care. You eat one immediately. The blueberries are burst and perfect. This is a sign from God.

**21. Hardboiled Eggs & Salt Packet**  
Two eggs, pre-peeled and a little sweaty in their baggie. A single salt packet from a fast food restaurant. The eggs smell faintly of sulfur. You eat them anyway. Protein is protein.

**22. Taquitos (Microwaved, Now Cold)**  
Wrapped in a thin napkin. The grease has soaked through completely. You knew what you were getting into. The outside is chewy. The inside is vaguely chicken. You dip them in the remaining salt packet.

**23. Hostess Fruit Pie (Cherry)**  
That weird, unnaturally red filling. The crust is a little crumbly, a little greasy. The icing is a thin, cracked shell. It's perfect in every way. You eat it in four bites and immediately want another.

**24. Freeze-Dried Ice Cream Sandwich**  
A neapolitan brick of impossible crunch. It's not _good_, but it's _interesting_. It dissolves on your tongue into a weird powder. You're not sure if you liked it, but you finished it.

**25. Backpacker's Pantry Pad Thai**  
Just add boiling water. If you have boiling water. If you don't, you stare at the pouch sadly. Surprisingly serviceable. The peanut packet is crucial. Without the peanut packet, it's just sad noodles.

**26. Clif Bar (Cool Mint Chocolate)**  
Tastes like a healthier version of a Thin Mint. Acceptable. A little dry. You chew it slowly. It's dense. It sits in your stomach like a friendly brick.

**27. Instant Oatmeal Packets (Variety Pack)**  
You have Maple & Brown Sugar, Apples & Cinnamon, and the one nobody wants: Plain. You have three of them. They're all Plain. You weep softly.

**28. Dried Mango Slices**  
Chewy, tart, sweet. Feels fancy, even though it's just dried fruit. The bag is half full of little sugar crystals from where they rubbed together. You pour those into your mouth.

**29. MRE (Menu 4: Beef Stew)**  
A brown pouch containing a brown substance. The chunks are vaguely meat-shaped. The potatoes are suspiciously cube-like. Comes with a tiny bottle of Tabasco, a cracker pack, and a mysterious spoon. You use the whole Tabasco.

**30. Tactical Bacon**  
Pre-cooked, shelf-stable bacon. It's... bacon-shaped. It's fine. It's chewy. It's salty. It's not _bacon_, but it's _bacon-adjacent_. You accept this.

**31. Survival Tabs (Chocolate Flavor)**  
Tastes like compressed chalk and regret. Provides 100% of daily vitamins. One "serving" is four tabs. You will eat two and feel empty. You save the other two for later. Later comes and you dread it.

**32. Ranger Bar (Coconut)**  
Dense, chewy, and somehow both dry and oily. Designed to pack maximum calories into minimum space. You will only eat half. The other half haunts your pack for days.

**33. Mom's Frozen Enchiladas (Thawed)**  
Wrapped in foil with a sticky note that says "Don't forget to heat through!" in familiar handwriting. You have no way to heat them. You eat them cold. They're still good. You miss her.

**34. Gas Station Pizza Slices**  
From the spinning rack under the heat lamp. You bought two yesterday. They're room temperature now. The pepperoni has that slight shine. The cheese is a little stiff. You eat them backwards, crust first, saving the tip for last.

**35. The Last Bag of Chips From Your Apartment**  
Barbecue. The bag is almost empty, just crumbs and that one perfect small chip at the bottom. You find the chip. You eat it slowly. You pour the crumbs into your mouth. It's over.

**36. A Candy Bar You've Been Saving**  
A York Peppermint Pattie. It's a little melted and re-hardened. The chocolate is slightly bloomed with white streaks. Still good. Still perfect. You earned this.

**37. "Emergency" Instant Ramen**  
The cheap square kind. You crunched it up in the package and are eating it raw, shaking the seasoning packet in. You are a gremlin and you know it. Everyone knows it. You own it.

**38. Turkey & Swiss on Rye**  
Wrapped tightly in deli paper, slightly pressed. A classic. Comes with a tiny packet of mustard that you will inevitably lose in your pocket and find weeks later. The bread is a little stale. Still delicious.

**39. Veggie Wrap**  
Hummus, shredded carrot, spinach, and bell peppers in a sun-dried tomato tortilla. Gets a little soggy by day two, but still good. The healthiest thing anyone has seen in days. You feel virtuous eating it.

**40. Spicy Nacho Cheese Doritos**  
The bag is crushed. You will be eating crumbs by the end. Worth it. The cheese dust covers your fingers. You lick them clean. No regrets.

**41. Pop-Tarts (Frosted Strawberry)**  
Two in a foil pouch. One is broken. The frosting is slightly cracked. You toast them if you can. If you can't, you eat them cold and they're still amazing. The broken one counts as a snack.

**42. Canned Spaghetti-Os with Meatballs**  
A single can. No can opener. You stare at it. You contemplate your life choices. You find a rock. You make it work. They're cold and delicious and you have never been happier.

**43. A Bag of Baby Carrots**  
Someone's sad desk lunch. Still cold. Still crunchy. The satisfying snap echoes in the silent room. You feel like a rabbit. A very grateful rabbit.

**44. Granola Bar (Crumbled)**  
Opened already. Just crumbs in the wrapper. You pour them into your mouth like birdseed. It's fine. It's still oats and honey. You accept your fate.

**45. A Single Beer**  
Warm. In a brown paper bag. No label. It's some local IPA from a brewery that probably closed months ago. It's bitter. It's warm. It's the best thing you've ever tasted.

**46. Bottled Water (Half Empty)**  
Found in a cup holder. It's warm. It's been there for days. There's a little dust in the threads. You drink it like it's the finest wine. Hydration is hydration.

**47. A Slightly Melty Snickers**  
From someone's glove compartment. It's been there since before everything happened. It's soft. Really soft. The nougat gives weirdly. You eat it in two bites. Perfection.

**48. Costco Muffin (Chocolate, Massive)**  
The size of your face. In a plastic clamshell. It's day-old, which means it's still good for three more days. You will not finish this in one sitting. You will try. You will fail. You will have more for later.

**49. A Ziploc of Mixed Nuts**  
Shells included. Pistachios, walnuts, a few almonds. Someone packed these with love. You spend an hour cracking them with a rock. Each nut is a tiny victory. The shells pile up around you like a little monument.

**50. Kevin's Lunch**  
The whole bag. You found Kevin's backpack. Inside: A turkey sandwich (you already ate that), an apple, a bag of chips (crushed, you ate those too), a handwritten note from someone named "Jenna" that says "Have a good day! #Location 
On the island of Trinidad, off the coast of [[Venezuela]], there is [[The Math Vault of Trinidad]]. Around a series of caves and caverns in the area ([[Hollow Earth]]), there are several mathematical equations carved into rock. Anyone who looks at them, it feels like utter nonsense. 
These numbers are a [[God|Divine]] number, and the reason they look senseless to most [[Human]]s, is because they do not have the ability of [[Pattern Manipulation]], or they haven't achieved [[Enlightenment]] yet. The person who can solve these equations, or who can read them will be able to unlock an entrance into the [[Hollow Earth]]. 

Also allows connection with the aliens, which means that the hollow earth inhabitants are in kahoots with [[Alien]]s.

## Mission Profile

**Station:** The Pequod maintains a continuous loiter at 1,200 meters depth, approximately 60km from the last confirmed sighting coordinates. It performs a vertical ascent to 600m every 72 hours to refresh sensor sweeps.

**Engagement Criteria:**

1. Detection of seismic signature matching Ses-Syquamlich emergence (0.8-1.2 Richter, unique waveform)
2. Confirmation via three independent sensor systems
3. Target identification at >80% confidence
4. No surface vessels within 200km (collateral avoidance)

**Firing Solution:** The missile is ejected horizontally, ignites at 300m separation, and climbs to intercept trajectory. Detonation occurs at optimal standoff distance determined by real-time analysis of creature's position and depth.

**Post-Detonation Protocol:** The Pequod will immediately dive to maximum depth and maintain silent running for 30 days, then ascend to verify kill via:
- Absence of seismic signatures
- Tissue samples in water column
- Disruption of deep-sea food chain patterns
## Notable Incidents

- **2010 (Gulf of Aden incident)**: The Pequod tracked the Ses-Syquamlich for 14 hours following the Artificial Sun activation. Firing solution was acquired but overridden by Illuminati High Council when 27 naval vessels entered the area to investigate "piracy." The creature was not seen again for 18 months.
- **2014**: The submarine detected the creature emerging near the Mid-Atlantic Ridge and tracked it for 6 days before losing the trail in a hydrothermal vent field. Analysis suggests the Ses-Syquamlich may have multiple lairs connected through underwater cave systems.
- **2019**: A scheduled maintenance window was delayed by 4 months due to "unusual activity" in the patrol zone. When the support vessel arrived, the Pequod's logs showed it had fired a warning shot (5kt yield) to deter the creature from approaching surface shipping lanes. The Illuminati officially "does not confirm" this event.
