#Theory
this relates to the santiago theory of cognition, which is a theory developed on how cognition works, developed by a couple [[Scientists]] in [[Chile]]. Life cannot exist without cognition, and cognition cannot exist without life. So if something has cognition, it must have life, and if it has life, it must have cognition, and consciousness is just cognition on a spectrum. and cognition is the ability to react to one's enviroment. A plant has the ability to react to external stimuli, so it must be alive. This relates to Cognitive Interference, which is when you randomly have a very bad thought, like having a thought to drive off road when driving a car. If someone had all of their senses removed completely, they'd have no cognition, and they'd get a really really long spurt of cognition. In the state between [[Life]] and [[Death]], there exists a purgatory where you get a lot of evil evil thoughts.
