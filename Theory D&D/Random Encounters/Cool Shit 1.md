# d100 Loot: Things You'd Actually Want to Find

---

## 1-20: Junk, but Like, Fun Junk

**1. Bluetooth Speaker (73% Battery)**
Only plays "Africa" by Toto. Not on repeat—just the one song, then it turns off. You have to turn it back on. It will play "Africa" again.

**2. Half-Full Vape Pen (Strawberry Ice)**
No nicotine. No THC. Just flavored air. Makes you look cool for exactly 3.4 seconds before you realize it's empty.

**3. Slightly Crushed Bag of Cool Ranch Doritos**
The bag is open. The chips are crumbs. You will eat the crumbs. You will enjoy the crumbs.

**4. A Single Flip Flop (Left Foot)**
Size 10. It's wet. You don't know why it's wet. It will never dry.

**5. Carabiner with 47 Keys on It**
None of them are labeled. None of them open anything you'll ever find. You will carry them forever.

**6. "World's Best Boss" Mug**
It's chipped. There's old coffee residue inside. Someone wrote "NOT" in sharpie underneath "Boss."

**7. A Charger Cable That's Broken at the Exact Wrong Angle**
If you hold it *like this*, it works. You will hold it like this for hours. Your hand will cramp. You will never stop holding it like this.

**8. Roll of Toilet Paper (One Square Left)**
You know what you have to do. You will do it. You will think about this moment for the rest of your life.

**9. A Single Earbud (Right Side)**
It works. You will only hear the right channel of everything forever. You will adjust.

**10. Expired Coupon for 50¢ Off Cat Food**
You don't have a cat. You will never have a cat. You keep the coupon.

**11. Sunglasses with One Lens Popped Out**
You put them on. You look like a time traveler from a bad 90s movie. You commit to the bit.

**12. A Stress Ball in the Shape of a Brain**
It's covered in dust. Someone squeezed it a lot. You squeeze it. You understand them.

**13. Half a Pack of Mentos (The Fruit Kind)**
They're stale. They're chewy. They're still Mentos. You eat all of them immediately.

**14. A Stick of Gum Still in the Wrapper, But It's Been Through the Wash**
The wrapper is disintegrating. The gum is wet. You consider it. You put it in your pocket. It will stay there for weeks.

**15. Pocket Lint Collection (Ziploc Bag)**
Someone saved their lint. For what purpose? You don't know. You keep it. You add to it. You become the lint person.

**16. A Single AA Battery (Unknown Charge)**
You put it in something. It works. It will die in exactly 12 minutes. You will never find it again.

**17. "I Voted" Sticker (Year 2016)**
The sticker is still sticky. You put it on your shirt. Everyone who sees it judges you for different reasons.

**18. A Rock That Looks Like a Potato**
It's just a rock. It looks exactly like a potato. You will show it to everyone. They will not care as much as you do.

**19. A Tennis Ball That a Dog Definitely Loved**
It's slobbery. It's fuzzy. The dog is gone. You have the ball now. You don't know what to do with it. You keep it.

**20. One of Those Pens with Four Colors**
The blue is dead. The green is dead. The red is dead. The black works. You will only ever use black. This is fine.

## 21-40: Actually Useful Stuff (Mostly)

**21. Foam Sword (Slightly Bent)**
It's a foam sword from a LARP event. It's bent at a 15-degree angle. You can still hit things with it. It does 1 bludgeoning damage. It's very funny when you do.

**22. Hand-Crank Flashlight**
Works forever if you're willing to work for it. Your arm will get tired. The light is dim. It's better than nothing. It's always almost nothing.

**23. Portable CD Player with "Now That's What I Call Music! 47"**
The CD skips on track 3. Track 3 is "*NSYNC - Bye Bye Bye." You learn to love the skip. It becomes part of the song.

**24. A Full Pack of Cigarettes (Brand: "Lucky Strike")**
There's a note inside: "Quit while you're ahead." You don't know who left it. You don't smoke. You keep them anyway. Just in case.

**25. Pocket Knife with 17 Attachments**
Three of the attachments are just different sizes of Phillips head screwdrivers. The corkscrew is bent. The tweezers are missing. You will use it for everything.

**26. Zippo Lighter (No Fluid)**
It's a Zippo. It's empty. You carry it anyway. You flick it open and closed when you're thinking. It's a habit now. It's who you are.

**27. Water Bottle with a Built-in Filter**
The filter expired in 2019. The water still tastes better than the unfiltered water. You tell yourself it's fine. It's probably fine.

**28. A Handgun (Glock 19) with One Bullet**
The bullet is in the chamber. The safety is off. You don't know how it got there. You don't know who it was for. You have it now. You have decisions to make.

**29. A Handgun (Glock 19) with a Full Magazine**
There's a note taped to it: "For the deer." There are no deer here. You are not a hunter. You have a gun now.

**30. A Map of the City with 47 Locations Circled in Red**
None of the locations exist anymore. The map is from 2003. You go to where one used to be. It's a parking lot. You feel something. You don't know what.

**31. A Flip Phone (It Turns On)**
The battery is at 12%. There's one contact saved: "MOM." You call it. It's disconnected. You send a text anyway. "Hey Mom. It's me. I'm okay." No response. You try again tomorrow.

**32. A Solar-Powered Calculator**
It works. You do math you don't need to do. 2+2=4. Good. The world makes sense for a moment.

**33. A Bag of 73 Rubber Bands**
They're all different sizes. They're all tangled together. You will spend an hour untangling them. You will enjoy it. You will never use a single one.

**34. A Roll of Duct Tape (Silver, Classic)**
This is the most useful item in existence. You know this. You will fix everything with it. You will fix things that aren't broken. You are become duct tape.

**35. A Box of Matches (Mostly Used)**
There are 8 matches left. The striker strip is worn smooth. You will get 3 of them to light. You will make each one count.

**36. A Harmonica (Key of C)**
You don't know how to play harmonica. You will learn one song. The song will be terrible. You will play it constantly. Your friends will hate you. You will not stop.

**37. A Ski Mask (It's Summer)**
It's wool. It's hot. You wear it anyway. You look suspicious. You are suspicious. You commit to the bit.

**38. A Pair of Night Vision Goggles (Broken)**
When you turn them on, you see a faint green glow and then nothing. You wear them anyway. You pretend they work. You walk into walls. You are committed.

**39. A First Aid Kit (Mostly Empty)**
There's one band-aid (Frozen themed) and an expired tube of Neosporin. The band-aid has Elsa on it. You cut your finger. You put Elsa on it. You feel better.

**40. A Tent (Missing One Pole)**
You set it up. It's lopsided. It's a lean-to now. It's shelter. It's your home. You name it "The Leaning Tower of Tent."

## 41-60: Weird Shit (The Good Weird)

**41. A Vial of "Cursed Water"**
The label is handwritten. It says "Do Not Drink." You drink it. Nothing happens. You feel slightly disappointed. (DM Note: It's just water. The person who labeled it was a jerk.)

**42. A Single Dice (d20)**
It's slightly weighted. You roll it. It always lands on 7. You roll it again. 7. You start making life decisions based on this die. It's always 7. You are now a 7 person.

**43. A Journal Written in Cuneiform**
You can't read it. You show it to someone who can. They read one page and close it. They won't tell you what it said. They just hand it back and say "Don't." You keep it. You don't open it again. (DM Note: It's just a grocery list. The reader was being dramatic.)

**44. A Small Glass Jar Labeled "Ambient Mana - Fresh"**
You open it. Nothing happens. You wave your hand through it. Nothing happens. You forget about it. Three days later, you feel slightly more rested. You don't know why. (DM Note: Contains 1 charge of "feel like you had a good nap." One use.)

**45. A USB Drive Labeled "Virus 23.exe"**
You don't plug it in. You're not stupid. You keep it. You think about what's on it. You dream about it. You never plug it in. Good.

**46. A Pocket-Sized "Depression Override" Kit**
It's a small tin with a mirror inside and a note that says "Smile :)"
You look in the mirror. You smile. You feel stupid. You feel slightly better. (DM Note: Once per day, you can look in the mirror and gain advantage on your next saving throw against fear. You have to actually smile.)

**47. A Feather That Never Stops Moving**
It's just a feather. It's on the ground. It's blowing in the wind. You pick it up. It keeps moving. In your pocket. Slight vibrations. It never stops. You get used to it. (DM Note: It's just vibrating. Always. No one knows why.)

**48. A Sunglasses Case Containing a Single Stick of Deer Jerky**
The jerky is from 2017. It's still good. Jerky is eternal. You eat it. It tastes like the woods. It tastes like memories. It's good.

**49. A Cassette Tape Labeled "Heaven (2 seconds)"**
You find a tape player. You press play. Static. Then, for exactly two seconds, you hear something beautiful. Indescribable. You cry a little. You play it again. Just static. (DM Note: The tape now only plays static. You had to be there.)

**50. A Business Card for "Bhutanese Shadow Market - Ask for Bob"**
There's no number. No address. Just a little drawing of a monk. You put it in your wallet. You forget about it. Months later, you meet someone named Bob. (DM Note: When you meet an NPC named Bob, they know you have this card. They nod knowingly. Nothing happens.)

**51. A Small Pouch of "De-Calcification Herbs"**
The herbs smell like oregano. They taste like oregano. You put them on pizza. The pizza is good. You feel slightly more connected to the universe. (DM Note: If you cook with these, the meal grants 1 temporary HP. They're just really good herbs.)

**52. A Water Bottle Labeled "Esero Khuga's Personal Water - DO NOT DRINK"**
You drink it. It tastes like water. You feel fine. You feel... regenerative? No. You just feel hydrated. (DM Note: It's just water. But if you ever need to make a death save, you have advantage. Once. The bottle is now empty.)

**53. A Single Earbud That Lets You Hear the Spiritual Plane**
You put it in. You hear whispers. You can't understand them. You take it out. Silence. You put it back in. Whispers. You learn to live with both worlds. (DM Note: While wearing it, you have advantage on Perception checks to detect invisible creatures, but disadvantage on social checks because you're distracted by ghost gossip.)

**54. A Foam Sword That Glows Faintly When Danger Is Near**
It's still a foam sword. It still does 1 bludgeoning damage. But when something bad is within 60 feet, it glows. You carry it everywhere. You look ridiculous. You are safe. (DM Note: Functions as an alarm. The glow is very faint, so you have to be looking at it.)

**55. A Smart Dust Detector (Looks Like a Glow Stick)**
You snap it. It turns blue. It stays blue. You wave it around. Still blue. You put it away. You don't know what blue means. (DM Note: It detects smart dust. Blue means "present." It's always blue. Smart dust is everywhere. You live with this knowledge now.)

**56. A Single Mint Condition "Solar Plexus Clown Glider" Trading Card**
The card shows a disturbing image. You look away. You look back. It's less disturbing now. You look away again. You look back. It's just a clown. A weird clown. But just a clown. (DM Note: After viewing the card three times, it becomes a normal clown card. The memetic hazard is neutralized. You now have a slightly weird but safe trading card.)

**57. A Can of "Anti-Pharma Beans"**
The label says "No Big Pharma. Just Beans." You open it. They're beans. Good beans. You eat them. You feel morally superior. (DM Note: They're really good beans. Heal 1 HP. You feel like you stuck it to the man.)

**58. A Bag of "Soy-Free" Chips**
You eat them. You feel masculine. You feel powerful. You punch a wall. Your hand hurts. You realize masculinity is a construct. You eat another chip. (DM Note: No mechanical effect. Just really good chips.)

**59. A Mirror That Shows Your Soul**
You look in it. You see yourself. You look tired. You see a faint glow around you. It's blue. You don't know what that means. You look away. You look back. Still blue. (DM Note: The color indicates your soul state. Blue = normal. Green = healthy. Red = corrupted. Black = soulless. You have no idea what any of this means because you have no reference. You just know you're blue. That's fine.)

**60. A "RAB-Khuga" Action Figure (Still in Package)**
It's a grotesque little figure of a fleshy mass with too many eyes. It's weirdly cute. The package says "Ages 8+." You're not sure about that. You keep it on your dashboard. It watches the road for you. (DM Note: No mechanical effect. But sometimes, when you're driving, you swear the eyes move. They don't. Probably.)

## 61-80: Useful Magic Shit

**61. A Vibration-Tuned Tuning Fork (Key of F)**
When you strike it and hold it near a wound, the wound stops hurting for 1d4 hours. It doesn't heal. It just stops hurting. You can use it 3 times per day. (DM Note: Functions as a temporary painkiller. Does not stack with healing.)

**62. A Pocket-Sized "Hook Operator" Amplifier**
It's a small microphone that plugs into your phone. When you speak into it, your voice carries 10 feet further than it should. People listen slightly more intently. (DM Note: Advantage on Performance checks when used. Runs on phone battery.)

**63. A Water Sample from Dr. Emoto's Lab**
It's in a small vial. The label says "Frozen 2004 - Thawed 2024." You look at it. It's just water. But if you concentrate, you see faint crystals forming. They're beautiful. (DM Note: Once per day, you can meditate on the water for 10 minutes. You regain 1 spell slot of the lowest level you have. The water level doesn't go down. It just... is.)

**64. A Bag of "Mana-Infused Trail Mix"**
The M&Ms glow faintly. You eat a handful. You feel energized. You feel magical. You cast a cantrip without expending anything. (DM Note: One serving (handful) lets you cast one cantrip without using a spell slot. There are 3 servings left. After that, it's just trail mix.)

**65. A "Pattern Manipulator's Starter Kit"**
It's a coloring book and some crayons. The coloring book is full of complex patterns. You color one in. You feel like you understand something. You don't know what. (DM Note: After coloring for 10 minutes, you have advantage on your next Intelligence check. The crayons are washable.)

**66. A Pair of Glasses That Let You See Chakras**
You put them on. Everyone has colorful swirls in their torso. Some are brighter than others. You see your own. It's orange. You don't know what that means. (DM Note: You can see the general health and emotional state of creatures. Red = angry. Blue = calm. Green = sick. etc. You have to learn what they mean through experience.)

**67. A "Cognitohazard" Warning Sticker**
It's just a sticker. It has a spooky symbol on it. You put it on your laptop. Nothing happens. You look at it again. You feel... fine. (DM Note: The sticker is safe. But anyone who sees it instinctively knows not to look too long. It's a good conversation starter.)

**68. A Small Jellyfish in a Jar (Alive)**
The jar is labeled "Archon Parasite - Do Not Release." The jellyfish pulses faintly. It's kind of pretty. You feed it tiny crumbs. It seems to like you. (DM Note: Once per day, you can concentrate on the jellyfish. It shows you what it "sees"—vague impressions of nearby creatures' emotional states. It's not precise, but it's something.)

**69. A "Virus 23" Resistant Face Mask**
It's a cloth mask with a complex pattern printed on it. You wear it. You feel protected. You don't know if it works. You wear it anyway. (DM Note: Grants advantage on saving throws against memetic hazards while worn. It looks ridiculous. You look like a cyberpunk wizard.)

**70. A Single Bullet Labeled "For the Ses-Syquamlich"**
It's a large caliber round. There's a note attached: "Just in case." You have no gun that takes this round. You carry it anyway. You dream of giant sea monsters. (DM Note: If you ever find a gun that fires this, it deals double damage to creatures of the "Cryptid" type. It's one bullet. Make it count.)

**71. A "Regenerative Death Consumption" Cookbook**
It's a small, handwritten recipe book. The recipes are... concerning. "Soul Stew." "Lifespan Lasagna." You don't make any of them. You keep it as a curiosity. (DM Note: Reading the cookbook is unsettling. But if you ever need to know about ritualistic cannibalism, this is the go-to source. Advantage on related knowledge checks.)

**72. A Small Bottle of "Ichor" (Label Says: "The God's Blood - 100% Water")**
You open it. It's water. You drink it. It's the best water you've ever had. You feel connected to everything. (DM Note: Drinking this grants 1d4 temporary HP and the overwhelming sense that you are part of something bigger. It lasts for 1 hour. The bottle now contains regular water.)

**73. A "Bank of Souls" ATM Card**
It's a black card with a tree on it. There's no name. You try to use it at an ATM. It says "INSUFFICIENT SOULS." You put it away. You try again next week. Same message. (DM Note: This card tracks your soul status. It's useless unless you know how to deposit souls. You don't.)

**74. A Pocket-Sized "Ascension Blacklist"**
It's a small notebook with names. Thousands of names. You don't recognize any. You add your name at the bottom in pencil. You cross it out. You add it again. (DM Note: Anyone who writes their name in this book has disadvantage on death saves. Anyone who crosses their name out has advantage. You can only do one or the other. Choose wisely.)

**75. A "Smart Dust" Activated Glow Stick**
You snap it. It turns red. You wave it around. It stays red. You put it in your pocket. Later, you feel strange. You check the glow stick. It's purple now. You don't know what that means. (DM Note: The glow stick changes color based on smart dust concentration in the area. Red = high. Blue = low. Purple = active sleeper agent nearby. You now have a paranoia tool.)

**76. A "Femboy Transmutation" Ring**
It's a simple silver ring. You put it on. You feel slightly more feminine. You take it off. You feel normal. You put it on again. You like it. (DM Note: While worn, you have advantage on Performance and Persuasion checks. You also feel very comfortable with your gender expression. It's a nice ring.)

**77. A "Paleolithic Deep State" Membership Card**
It's a small card with a Neanderthal on it. The text is in an ancient language. You show it to people. They don't recognize it. You keep it. Maybe one day. (DM Note: If you ever meet a Neanderthal or a member of the Paleolithic Deep State, they recognize this card. They treat you as neutral instead of hostile. One use.)

**78. A "CIA Vaccine" Band-Aid**
It's a normal band-aid. But the wrapper says "Experimental - Soul Removal." You put it on a cut. Nothing happens. You feel fine. You wonder. (DM Note: It's a normal band-aid. But anyone who sees it is slightly unsettled. You lean into it. You become the unsettling person.)

**79. A Small Terrarium with a Piece of the "RAB-Khuga" Biomass**
It's a tiny, fleshy lump. It pulses slightly. It has one small eye that blinks at you sometimes. You feed it crumbs. It seems to like you. (DM Note: It's a tiny piece of the cryptid. It's harmless. It grows very slowly. After a year, it might be the size of a fist. It's your friend now. It doesn't do anything. It just is.)

**80. A "Ses-Syquamlich" Tooth (Fossilized)**
It's a massive tooth, the size of your forearm. It's heavy. It's sharp. You carry it as a weapon. It deals 1d8 damage and counts as magical for overcoming resistance. (DM Note: It's a +0 weapon. But it's a giant monster tooth. That's cool as hell.)

## 81-100: The Good Shit (Active/Lore Items)

**81. The "Phantom Time" Wristwatch**
It's a vintage watch. It runs 300 years slow. You set it to the correct time. It immediately resets. You learn to live on phantom time. (DM Note: Once per day, you can look at the watch and ask "Is this the real timeline?" The DM must tell you if you're in a historical inaccuracy. Useless but fascinating.)

**82. A Foam Sword That Becomes Real When You Yell "FOR NARNIA!"**
It's a foam sword. You yell the phrase. It becomes a real longsword (+1) for 1 minute. You can do this once per day. The foam sword is now slightly worn. (DM Note: It returns to foam after the minute. You look very silly charging into battle with a foam sword, then suddenly having a real one.)

**83. A "Pattern Manipulator's Abacus"**
It's a small wooden abacus. When you move the beads, they form patterns. You can solve complex equations in your head. (DM Note: While using the abacus, you have advantage on any Intelligence check involving math, puzzles, or patterns. It's very satisfying to click.)

**84. A Small Glass Sphere Containing a "Hyper Beast" (Dormant)**
It's a tiny creature, sleeping. It looks like a small, furry thing with too many eyes. It's cute. It's sleeping. You keep it on your nightstand. It purrs sometimes. (DM Note: Once per week, you can wake it. It grants you advantage on one saving throw. Then it goes back to sleep. It's a good friend.)

**85. A "Cognitohazard" Camouflage Poncho**
It's a poncho covered in patterns that hurt to look at. When you wear it, people look away. They don't know why. They just... don't want to look at you. (DM Note: While wearing the poncho, you have advantage on Stealth checks in crowds. Disadvantage on social checks because people find you unsettling.)

**86. A "Solar Plexus Clown Glider" in a Jar**
It's a small, flying creature. It looks like a tiny clown with translucent wings. It's trapped in a jar. You let it out. It flies around your head. It follows you. It's your friend now. It honks sometimes. (DM Note: It's a familiar. It can't do much, but it's a tiny flying clown. It honks when danger is near. You love it.)

**87. A "RAB-Khuga" Regenerative Flesh Sample (In Stasis Jar)**
The jar is cold. Inside, a piece of flesh pulses. It's trying to grow. You feed it. It grows. You now have a pet flesh mass. It's weird. It's yours. (DM Note: Once per day, you can ask the flesh to heal a wound. It extends a tendril and touches you. You regain 1d4 HP. It's gross. It works.)

**88. A "Bank of Souls" Credit Card**
This one works. You can use it to pay for things. But every time you do, you feel slightly... emptier. (DM Note: The card spends your soul as currency. For every 100 euros spent, you lose 1 maximum HP permanently. It's a bad deal. But if you're desperate...)

**89. A "Virus 23" Resistant Hard Drive**
It's a small, rugged hard drive. It contains backups of your consciousness. If you die, someone can plug this in and upload you to a new body. (DM Note: If you die and your party recovers the drive, you can come back as an AI construct or be resurrected with the drive as a component. One use. Very valuable.)

**90. A "Ses-Syquamlich" Caller**
It's a small, waterproof speaker. When activated, it plays a frequency that attracts the giant cryptid. You have 1d4 rounds before it arrives. (DM Note: This is a panic button. You will probably die. But if you're on a boat and you want to summon a 400-foot monster, this is your item.)

**91. The "Pequod" Remote Control**
It's a small remote with one button. The button says "LAUNCH." You press it. Nothing happens. You press it again. Nothing. (DM Note: This remote is keyed to the Pequod submarine. If you're ever within 100 miles of it, pressing this will launch the nuclear missile. It's a one-use "fuck everything in that direction" button. Use wisely.)

**92. A "Hollow Earth" Elevator Pass**
It's a keycard. There's no label. But when you're in an elevator, you can swipe it. The elevator goes down. Past the basement. Past the sub-basement. Past where it should stop. (DM Note: This takes you to the Hollow Earth. It's a one-way trip unless you have another way back. Use with caution.)

**93. A "Math Vault of Trinidad" Equation Card**
It's a small card with a complex equation on it. You can't solve it. But when you show it to someone good at math, their eyes glaze over. They understand. They won't tell you what it means. (DM Note: This card, when solved, reveals a path to the Hollow Earth or a way to contact the beings there. It's a plot hook. It's also a really cool card.)

**94. A "Stone Head of Guatemala" Miniature**
It's a small stone head, about the size of your fist. It looks ancient. Sometimes, it opens its eyes. It looks at you. It closes them. You don't talk about it. (DM Note: The head gives you advice. It's always cryptic. It's never wrong. Once per week, you can ask it one question. The DM gives you a cryptic but accurate answer.)

**95. A "Real Humans Died Millennia Ago" Fossil**
It's a fossilized bone. It's humanoid. But it's... wrong. Too many joints. Too many fingers. You keep it. You dream of giants. (DM Note: The fossil hums with ancient power. While carrying it, you have resistance to necrotic damage. You also have weird dreams about the previous cycle.)

**96. A "Templars Order" Relic (The Key of Solomon)**
It's a small, ornate key. It doesn't open any lock you've found. But when you hold it, you feel like you understand ancient secrets. (DM Note: The key grants you the ability to read any ancient language. Cuneiform, hieroglyphs, whatever. It works. It's amazing. You are now the party's translator.)

**97. A "Paleolithic Deep State" Alliance Totem**
It's a small, carved figure of a Neanderthal. When you hold it, you feel connected to the ancient ones. (DM Note: Once per day, you can summon a spectral Neanderthal warrior. It fights for you for 1 minute. It's a spirit. It's strong. It's your friend.)

**98. A "Vatican Vault" Access Card (Stolen)**
It's a black card with a Vatican seal. It's warm to the touch. You don't know what it opens. But you know it opens something important. (DM Note: This card grants access to the Vatican Vault. If you can get there. And if you can survive what's inside. Good luck.)

**99. A "God's Ego Death" Shard**
It's a small piece of... something. It's not stone. It's not metal. It's not anything you recognize. It hums with power. It's a piece of the divine. (DM Note: This shard can be used to cast Wish once. After using it, it crumbles to dust. It's the most powerful item you'll ever hold. Don't waste it.)

**100. A "The God" Fragment (A Single Drop of Water)**
It's a single drop of water, floating in a small vial. It's perfect. It's everything. You look at it. You see the universe. (DM Note: Drinking this grants you one level of Enlightenment. You become a Pattern Manipulator. You gain the ability to cast a single 9th-level spell of your choice, once. After that, you're just a normal person who drank god-water. But for that one moment... you were everything.)
