THE D100

**1. The Mystery Can**  
No label. No idea. Could be soup. Could be cat food. Could be beets. Could be peaches. The only way to know is to open it. (DM decides later, or have them roll again on this table when they open it.)

**2. Warhead Sour Candy (Extreme)**  
A roll of six. The green ones are the worst. Your face does an involuntary thing. Your mouth will regret this tomorrow. Worth it.

**3. A Single Pickle**  
In a Ziploc baggie. Still floating in a little juice. Dill. Surprisingly crisp. Who saves one pickle?

**4. Saltine Cracker Sleeve**  
Crushed to absolute dust. You have a packet of saltine dust. You could make a slurry. You could inhale it. No good options.

**5. Grandma's Oatmeal Cookies**  
In a tin with flowers on it. Slightly too much cinnamon. They're falling apart a little, crumbs at the bottom. They're perfect. You feel guilty eating the last one.

**6. Gas Station Sushi**  
A terrible idea. A truly terrible idea. Why did anyone buy this? It's been in the roller case for hours, then sat overnight. The rice is weird. The fish is a suggestion. Con save to keep it down.

**7. Beef Jerky (Peppered)**  
Aggressively salty, aggressively chewy. Could double as a weapon in a pinch. The pepper flakes get stuck in your teeth for hours.

**8. Protein Bar (Cookies & Cream)**  
Tastes like candy pretending to be health food. Slightly chalky, but it'll keep you going. The white coating gets on your fingers.

**9. Honey Bun (Glazed)**  
Individually wrapped, impossibly sweet. Leaves a film of sugar on your fingers. The spiral is perfectly imperfect. A classic.

**10. Tuna Salad Kit**  
A small can of tuna, a separate packet of mayonnaise, a tiny plastic spoon, and a sleeve of crackers. Requires assembly. Smells exactly as you'd expect. Other party members will know what you're eating from three rooms away.

**11. MRE (Menu Unknown)**  
The label is gone. The pouch is olive drab. You open it. It's brown. It's fine. Comes with a tiny bottle of Tabasco, a cracker pack, and a spoon. The Tabasco is essential.

**12. Trail Mix (The Good Kind)**  
M&Ms, peanuts, cashews, raisins, and those little yogurt-covered pretzels. Everyone picks out the M&Ms first. Someone has already picked out the M&Ms. There are none left. You have been betrayed by a stranger.

**13. Peanut Butter Crackers**  
The kind with six little sandwiches and the plastic spreading knife. The knife is useless. You eat them in three bites. The peanut butter sticks to the roof of your mouth.

**14. An Actual Apple**  
Red Delicious. Slightly bruised. Real. Fresh. Crisp. This is a treasure beyond measure. You eat the whole thing, core included, like an animal.

**15. Bag of Oranges**  
Three of them. The net bag is torn. One is slightly soft. One is perfect. The smell alone lifts everyone's spirits. Vitamin C has never been so precious.

**16. Leftover Pizza Box**  
From two days ago. One slice left. Cheese. It's cold. The cheese has that slightly plasticky texture. It's still pizza. It's still good. You eat it standing up.

**17. A Full Sandwich (Kevin's)**  
Wrapped in deli paper with a name written in sharpie. "KEVIN." Turkey and Swiss on rye, lettuce, tomato, a little mustard. You don't know Kevin. Thanks, Kevin. You think about Kevin sometimes.

**18. MRE (Chili Mac)**  
Widely considered one of the better ones. Still brown. Still comes with Tabasco. The noodles are tiny. The chili is more of a suggestion. It's hot. It's food. It's okay.

**19. A Can of Tuna and One Packet of Crackers**  
The crackers are the kind that come with soup. Four tiny rectangles. You will make this work. You eat the tuna with the crackers, carefully rationing each cracker for maximum coverage.

**20. Freshly Baked Muffins**  
In a paper bag, still warm. Blueberry. Three of them. How? Why? Who left these? You don't care. You eat one immediately. The blueberries are burst and perfect. This is a sign from God.

**21. Hardboiled Eggs & Salt Packet**  
Two eggs, pre-peeled and a little sweaty in their baggie. A single salt packet from a fast food restaurant. The eggs smell faintly of sulfur. You eat them anyway. Protein is protein.

**22. Taquitos (Microwaved, Now Cold)**  
Wrapped in a thin napkin. The grease has soaked through completely. You knew what you were getting into. The outside is chewy. The inside is vaguely chicken. You dip them in the remaining salt packet.

**23. Hostess Fruit Pie (Cherry)**  
That weird, unnaturally red filling. The crust is a little crumbly, a little greasy. The icing is a thin, cracked shell. It's perfect in every way. You eat it in four bites and immediately want another.

**24. Freeze-Dried Ice Cream Sandwich**  
A neapolitan brick of impossible crunch. It's not _good_, but it's _interesting_. It dissolves on your tongue into a weird powder. You're not sure if you liked it, but you finished it.

**25. Backpacker's Pantry Pad Thai**  
Just add boiling water. If you have boiling water. If you don't, you stare at the pouch sadly. Surprisingly serviceable. The peanut packet is crucial. Without the peanut packet, it's just sad noodles.

**26. Clif Bar (Cool Mint Chocolate)**  
Tastes like a healthier version of a Thin Mint. Acceptable. A little dry. You chew it slowly. It's dense. It sits in your stomach like a friendly brick.

**27. Instant Oatmeal Packets (Variety Pack)**  
You have Maple & Brown Sugar, Apples & Cinnamon, and the one nobody wants: Plain. You have three of them. They're all Plain. You weep softly.

**28. Dried Mango Slices**  
Chewy, tart, sweet. Feels fancy, even though it's just dried fruit. The bag is half full of little sugar crystals from where they rubbed together. You pour those into your mouth.

**29. MRE (Menu 4: Beef Stew)**  
A brown pouch containing a brown substance. The chunks are vaguely meat-shaped. The potatoes are suspiciously cube-like. Comes with a tiny bottle of Tabasco, a cracker pack, and a mysterious spoon. You use the whole Tabasco.

**30. Tactical Bacon**  
Pre-cooked, shelf-stable bacon. It's... bacon-shaped. It's fine. It's chewy. It's salty. It's not _bacon_, but it's _bacon-adjacent_. You accept this.

**31. Survival Tabs (Chocolate Flavor)**  
Tastes like compressed chalk and regret. Provides 100% of daily vitamins. One "serving" is four tabs. You will eat two and feel empty. You save the other two for later. Later comes and you dread it.

**32. Ranger Bar (Coconut)**  
Dense, chewy, and somehow both dry and oily. Designed to pack maximum calories into minimum space. You will only eat half. The other half haunts your pack for days.

**33. Mom's Frozen Enchiladas (Thawed)**  
Wrapped in foil with a sticky note that says "Don't forget to heat through!" in familiar handwriting. You have no way to heat them. You eat them cold. They're still good. You miss her.

**34. Gas Station Pizza Slices**  
From the spinning rack under the heat lamp. You bought two yesterday. They're room temperature now. The pepperoni has that slight shine. The cheese is a little stiff. You eat them backwards, crust first, saving the tip for last.

**35. The Last Bag of Chips From Your Apartment**  
Barbecue. The bag is almost empty, just crumbs and that one perfect small chip at the bottom. You find the chip. You eat it slowly. You pour the crumbs into your mouth. It's over.

**36. A Candy Bar You've Been Saving**  
A York Peppermint Pattie. It's a little melted and re-hardened. The chocolate is slightly bloomed with white streaks. Still good. Still perfect. You earned this.

**37. "Emergency" Instant Ramen**  
The cheap square kind. You crunched it up in the package and are eating it raw, shaking the seasoning packet in. You are a gremlin and you know it. Everyone knows it. You own it.

**38. Turkey & Swiss on Rye**  
Wrapped tightly in deli paper, slightly pressed. A classic. Comes with a tiny packet of mustard that you will inevitably lose in your pocket and find weeks later. The bread is a little stale. Still delicious.

**39. Veggie Wrap**  
Hummus, shredded carrot, spinach, and bell peppers in a sun-dried tomato tortilla. Gets a little soggy by day two, but still good. The healthiest thing anyone has seen in days. You feel virtuous eating it.

**40. Spicy Nacho Cheese Doritos**  
The bag is crushed. You will be eating crumbs by the end. Worth it. The cheese dust covers your fingers. You lick them clean. No regrets.

**41. Pop-Tarts (Frosted Strawberry)**  
Two in a foil pouch. One is broken. The frosting is slightly cracked. You toast them if you can. If you can't, you eat them cold and they're still amazing. The broken one counts as a snack.

**42. Canned Spaghetti-Os with Meatballs**  
A single can. No can opener. You stare at it. You contemplate your life choices. You find a rock. You make it work. They're cold and delicious and you have never been happier.

**43. A Bag of Baby Carrots**  
Someone's sad desk lunch. Still cold. Still crunchy. The satisfying snap echoes in the silent room. You feel like a rabbit. A very grateful rabbit.

**44. Granola Bar (Crumbled)**  
Opened already. Just crumbs in the wrapper. You pour them into your mouth like birdseed. It's fine. It's still oats and honey. You accept your fate.

**45. A Single Beer**  
Warm. In a brown paper bag. No label. It's some local IPA from a brewery that probably closed months ago. It's bitter. It's warm. It's the best thing you've ever tasted.

**46. Bottled Water (Half Empty)**  
Found in a cup holder. It's warm. It's been there for days. There's a little dust in the threads. You drink it like it's the finest wine. Hydration is hydration.

**47. A Slightly Melty Snickers**  
From someone's glove compartment. It's been there since before everything happened. It's soft. Really soft. The nougat gives weirdly. You eat it in two bites. Perfection.

**48. Costco Muffin (Chocolate, Massive)**  
The size of your face. In a plastic clamshell. It's day-old, which means it's still good for three more days. You will not finish this in one sitting. You will try. You will fail. You will have more for later.

**49. A Ziploc of Mixed Nuts**  
Shells included. Pistachios, walnuts, a few almonds. Someone packed these with love. You spend an hour cracking them with a rock. Each nut is a tiny victory. The shells pile up around you like a little monument.

**50. Kevin's Lunch**  
The whole bag. You found Kevin's backpack. Inside: A turkey sandwich (you already ate that), an apple, a bag of chips (crushed, you ate those too), a handwritten note from someone named "Jenna" that says "Have a good day! Love you!" and a slightly sweaty cheese stick. You have consumed Kevin's entire identity. You are Kevin now. This is your life.

**51. A Single Gummy Worm**
Just one. In a Ziploc. Red. Someone bit the head off already. The bite mark is old. You stare at it for a long time. You eat it. It's chewy. You think about the head.

**52. Monster Energy (The Green One)**
Warm. Has been rolling around on the floor of someone's car for weeks. The can is dented. You open it. It still fizzes. It tastes like liquid regret and caffeine. You feel alive again.

**53. A Jar of Pickles (Unopened)**
Full gallon size. Someone's bulk purchase. You have no idea how to carry this. You want to. You really want to. You take three pickles out and leave the jar. You will think about the remaining pickles for days.

**54. Gas Station Hot Dog**
Found under the heat lamps. It has been there for an unknown duration. The skin is shiny. The bun is steamed into submission. The roller marks are permanent. You add ketchup from a crusty pump. This is a gamble and you know it.

**55. A Single Starburst (Pink)**
Individually wrapped. Slightly melted. Re-hardened into a weird shape. It's the best one. Someone knew what they were saving. Now it's yours.

**56. Mystery Thermos**
You open it. You smell it. You make a decision. Roll a d4:
1. Coffee. Still warm. How?
2. Soup. Chunky. What kind? The world may never know.
3. Just water. Disappointing.
4. Something that used to be alive. You close it. You do not open it again.

**57. Fast Food Ketchup Packets**
A handful. Like, twenty of them. From five different restaurants. Someone just grabbed a fistful once. You are now the ketchup king. You didn't ask for this power.

**58. A Single Pringle**
At the bottom of a tube. It's broken. It's the perfect Pringle shape but in three pieces. You assemble them in your mouth. It counts.

**59. Gatorade (Blue, Half Full)**
Found in a gym bag. The cap is sticky. There's gym dust floating in it. It's warm. It's electric blue. You drink it. It tastes like victory and Staphylococcus.

**60. A Block of Cheese (Still in Wrapper)**
Like, a full block of cheddar. Someone's grocery run abandoned. It's sweating slightly. It's fine. Cheese is cheese. You cut off the sweaty part. You are now a cheese person.

**61. Canned Bread**
Yes, this is a real thing. Someone had canned bread. Why? For what purpose? You open it. It's... bread-shaped. In a can. You eat it. It's bread. You have questions you will never get answers to.

**62. A Ziploc of Only Croutons**
Just croutons. No salad. No soup. Someone packed croutons as a snack. You respect their energy. They're still crunchy. You eat them like chips.

**63. Energy Drink (The One Nobody Likes)**
Venom. Rip-It. Some off-brand with a skull on it. It costs 99 cents new. It tastes like battery acid and ambition. You drink it anyway. You now have wings made of regret.

**64. A Banana Peel**
Just the peel. In a bag. Like someone saved it. For what purpose? Compost? A prank? You don't know. You throw it away. You wonder about the person who saved a banana peel.

**65. Slightly Used Gum**
Still in the wrapper. Only chewed for like, five minutes. Someone's discarded gum. You find it in a backpack pocket. You consider it. You decide against it. Mostly.

**66. A Can of Beets**
Nobody wants this. Nobody has ever wanted this. You found a can of beets. You carry it for three days before abandoning it. You will find another can of beets later. The universe is mocking you.

**67. French Onion Dip (Only the Dip)**
A tub of dip. No chips. No vegetables. Just dip. Someone's priorities were clear. You eat it with a spoon. You are them now. You understand them.

**68. Bottle of Hot Sauce**
Just hot sauce. Like, a full bottle. Someone loved hot sauce. You put it on everything. You become the hot sauce person. You run out in a week. You mourn.

**69. A Single Onion**
Just an onion. Rolling around in a glove compartment. It's starting to sprout. The green shoot is optimistic. You eat it anyway. It's spicy. It's crunchy. It's trying its best.

**70. Half a Sandwich (Peanut Butter and Fluff)**
The other half is missing. There are teeth marks. Someone ate the other half and saved this one. For later. Later never came. You finish their journey for them.

**71. Mystery Soda (Store Brand)**
Dr. Thunder. Mr. Cola. Dr. Perky. Some knockoff. It's flat. It's warm. It tastes like the idea of soda. You drink it and feel nothing.

**72. A Bag of Frozen Peas (Thawed)**
Someone's ice pack. Now it's just pea soup in a bag. You eat them cold. They're mushy. They're still peas. You have made worse decisions.

**73. A Single Marshmallow**
Stale. Like, really stale. It's hard. It's crunchy. It's not what a marshmallow should be. You eat it anyway. It crumbles in your mouth like sugary dust.

**74. Fast Food Napkins with Someone's Phone Number**
Written in pen. "Call me, maybe?" A name: Tiffany. Or maybe it says "Tiffani." The handwriting is hopeful. You keep the napkin. You wonder about Tiffany. You never call.

**75. A Can of Mixed Vegetables**
The saddest can. The vegetables are tiny cubes. The liquid is viscous. You heat it up. It's still sad. You eat it. You are sad. The vegetables are also sad. It's a whole sad ecosystem.

**76. A Half-Eaten Candy Necklace**
The string is still there. Someone ate most of the candy discs. The last three are still on the string. They're dusty. They're delicious.

**77. Kombucha (Homemade, Exploded)**
The bottle is bulging. The cap is stressed. There are bubbles inside. You open it carefully. It fountains everywhere. What's left is vinegary and weird. You drink it. Your gut flora is confused.

**78. A Single Twinkie**
Individually wrapped. It's ancient. Twinkies are immortal. This one will outlive us all. You eat it. It tastes like the apocalypse. Spongey.

**79. Mystery Meat Stick**
No label. Waxy exterior. Found in a coat pocket. Is it pepperoni? Is it something else? You sniff it. It smells like meat. It smells like salt. It smells like a question. You eat it. The question remains unanswered.

**80. A Ziploc of Cereal (The Sugary Kind)**
Froot Loops. Trix. Something colorful. Someone poured cereal into a baggie for the road. It's crushed into dust and colorful crumbs. You pour it into your mouth. It's sugar dust. It's perfect.

**81. Baby Food Pouch (Squished)**
Apples and blueberries. Someone's toddler food. The pouch is half empty and squished into a backpack. You squeeze the rest into your mouth. It's actually good. You feel weird about it.

**82. A Single Cheese String**
Already peeled. Like, someone started peeling it and stopped. It's a cheese string tree. You finish the peeling. Each string is a tiny victory. You eat them one by one.

**83. Expired Protein Shake**
Two months past. "Shake well" it says. You shake it. It's chunky. You open it. You smell it. You decide life is about taking risks. It's fine. It's terrible. You finish it.

**84. A Cookie (Homemade, Burned on the Bottom)**
Someone tried. They really tried. The top is perfect. The bottom is charcoal. You scrape the black off. The middle is still good. You appreciate the effort.

**85. Bottled Water (Opened, with a Note)**
"I didn't poison this. Probably. - Dave." You don't know Dave. Dave is a stranger. Dave left you water. You trust Dave. You drink Dave's water. Thanks, Dave.

**86. A Can of Sardines**
The key is still attached. You wind it. The smell escapes. It's aggressive. It's fishy. It's love at first scent. You eat them with your fingers. Oil everywhere. Worth it.

**87. A Single Oreo (Double Stuf)**
Double Stuf. The good kind. Someone saved the best for last. It's a little broken. One side has more cream than the other. You twist it. You lick the cream. You eat the cookie. You honor their memory.

**88. Gas Station Cinnamon Roll**
In plastic. The icing is a separate packet. The roll is dense. The icing is nuclear white. You apply the entire packet. It's too much icing. There's no such thing as too much icing.

**89. A Bag of Salad (Just the Salad)** 
No dressing. No croutons. No vegetables besides lettuce. It's just leaves. Sad, wilting leaves. You eat them like a disappointed rabbit. You need fiber. You get fiber.

**90. A Single Funyun**
At the bottom of a bag. The bag is empty except for this one Funyun. It's the last survivor. It's slightly stale. It's perfectly oniony. You salute it before eating.

**91. Mystery Liquid in a Gatorade Bottle**
The bottle is blue Gatorade. The liquid inside is brown. You open it. You smell it. Roll a d6:
1. Tea. Sweet tea. Everything's fine.
2. Gasoline. You almost made a terrible mistake.
3. Urine. You definitely made a terrible mistake.
4. Apple juice. Confusing, but fine.
5. Oil. Motor oil. You close it.
6. Moonshine. You just found a treasure. Sip carefully.

**92. A Half-Bag of Marshmallows**
The rest of the bag is empty. Someone ate all the good ones. These are the tiny ones. The ones for hot chocolate. You don't have hot chocolate. You eat them plain. They're fine.

**93. A Can of Spam**
The whole can. Unopened. Spam is eternal. Spam is love. Spam is life. You open it. The block slides out. It's perfectly pink. You slice it with a pocket knife. You fry it if you can. You eat it raw if you can't. Spam doesn't care.

**94. A Single Packet of Mayo**
Just one. From a deli. Someone took one mayo packet. For what? One sandwich? One desperate moment? You find it in a pocket. You save it. You never use it. It follows you for weeks.

**95. A Granola Bar (Opened, One Bite Missing)**
Someone took one bite. Just one. And then put it back. Why? Did they not like it? Were they saving it? Did something happen? You eat the rest. You finish their story. It was pretty good.

**96. A Bottle of Maple Syrup (The Real Kind)**
Not the corn syrup stuff. The real amber magic. In a glass bottle. Someone's fancy purchase. You put it on everything. Everything. You run out in two days. You grieve.

**97. A Ziploc of Powdered Donut Holes**
They were donut holes once. Now they're just donut-shaped dust collectors. The powdered sugar has become one with the bag. You reach in. You pull out sugary mush. You eat it. It's still donut.

**98. A Single Cracker (Fish-Shaped)**
Goldfish. Something like that. One cracker. At the bottom of a backpack. It's crushed but intact. It smiles at you. Its little fish face knows nothing of the horrors. You eat it. It knew everything.

**99. Kevin's Second Lunch**
You find another backpack. Same brand. Same worn patches. Inside: A sandwich (ham and cheese, slightly moldy on one corner—you cut that part off), a bag of chips (barbecue, mostly crumbs), an apple (bruised but perfect), and a note: "Kev, don't forget we're meeting at 6. - J." Kevin has two backpacks. Kevin lives multiple lives. Kevin is everywhere and nowhere. You eat Kevin's second lunch. You are Kevin's companion now. There are two of you.

**100. The Perfect Item**
You open the container. You smell it. You see it. It's exactly what you needed right now. Not what you wanted. What you needed. Roll a d4:
1. A cold bottle of your favorite drink. Condensation running down the sides. It's perfect.
2. A warm meal that tastes like something from your childhood. Your mom's cooking. Your grandma's recipe. You cry a little.
3. A piece of fruit so fresh and perfect it feels like a dream. You bite into it. Juice runs down your chin. You close your eyes.
4. Nothing. The container is empty. But you feel full anyway. Hope, maybe. You'll take it.
